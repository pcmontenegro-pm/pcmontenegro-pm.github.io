I have 10+ years in banking and financial services — 7 of them at Itaú Chile, one of Latin America's largest banks, owning the product roadmap for digital payments and collections. I built PAC 2.0, led QR interoperability programs, delivered ePAC (the digital mandate platform), and managed squads across payment infrastructure and operational tooling. Since then, I've worked as an independent payments consultant for fintech teams across LatAm and Europe.

I moved to Dublin because the same problems I solved in Chile exist in Europe. The SEPA infrastructure is more sophisticated. The regulatory framework is clearer. But the gap between what the data says and what businesses actually do with it? Identical.

I understand the full stack: APIs, payment rails, failure modes, retry logic, mandate management, notification engines, and what the rejection file actually says. I don't just know how payments work — I know why they fail, and what to do about it.
